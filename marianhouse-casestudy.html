Marian House Website ReDesign Case Study

Role: UX Designer

Marian House was designed to house and care for women in Baltimore City. The current donation product is not promoting trust through their online presence, which is causing potential donors to opt out of giving to Marian House. By promoting trust and elevating transparency, users will increase monetary donations to Mariar House. In a team of 4, my responsibilities were:
  • User Interviews
  • Iterating User Flow
  • Wireframes
  • Prototyping
  • Responsive Web Design
Design I hinking Process

Based on the interviews an affinity diagram was used to visualize potential users that Marian House attracts. Penny Burtonstein was built as the user persona. She was referred throughout the entire product development process. The I like, What If Method was used to determine potential solutions for users such as Penny. Three key features were decided to improve Penny's experience donating to Marian House.

  • A secure donation process
  • A monetary breakdown of Penny's financial impact
  • A trustworthy, modern web presence
Wireframes

Lo-Fi wireframes testing had users donate $50 one time as a guest. Useberry results demonstrated 16 users completed the prototype task within 38.5 seconds.

User testing: Useberry

  • Metrics for success
  • Time
  • Misclick rate

From this testing, it was determined the donation process need to be redone to allow for a faster checkout without confusing users if they need to login or sign up as a donor.

Prototype

Outcomes of testing the Hi-Fi Prototype with the same task of donating saw a reduce average time of 26.9 seconds to complete the tasks. Users immediately saw the mission statement when entering the website, they were shown a board of live donations, and donation process gave users information on how their donations were being used at Marian House

User testing: Useberry

  • Metrics for sucess
  • Time
  • Misclick rate

For users like Penny, the redesign website gave donors more transparency with their donations, a secure donation process and a modern web presence. By using Material Design, and giving the primary and secondary colors Marian House uses today a softer tone, the redesign website displayed an innovated look to users.

What have you learned from this project

  • Time Constraint. With three weeks to complete the redesign, the top priority was the donation process

Overcome
